# ECP Best Practices: BIS Nine Elements and DDTC Alignment

Reference for building an Export Compliance Program (ECP) document. BIS has endorsed nine critical ECP elements; DDTC expects analogous program elements for ITAR-regulated activities. Use this to structure the ECP document template and tailor content to company footprint, product mix, and risk profile.

---

## 1. BIS nine ECP elements (detail)

### 1.1 Management commitment

- **What it is:** A signed, senior-level (e.g. CEO or CCO) policy statement committing the organization to export compliance.
- **What to document:** Statement that the company will comply with the EAR (and ITAR where applicable); designation of Export Compliance Officer (ECO) or equivalent; commitment to adequate resources and no retaliation for good-faith reporting.
- **ECP doc section:** Dedicated "Management Commitment" section with placeholder for signatory, title, and date.

### 1.2 Risk assessment

- **What it is:** Systematic assessment of product, operations, and customer risk (e.g. ECCN mix, destinations, end users, screening exposure).
- **What to document:** How the company profiles risk (product mix, geography, end-use); frequency of reassessment; who owns it.
- **ECP doc section:** "Risk Assessment" describing approach, triggers for re-assessment, and linkage to screening/classification/licensing.

### 1.3 Export authorization

- **What it is:** Written procedures that produce classification memos and license determinations (what authorization is needed and why).
- **What to document:** Classification process (Order of Review, CCL/USML, documentation); license determination process (five determinative facts, Country Chart, exceptions, formal license); who is responsible; where records are kept.
- **ECP doc section:** "Export Authorization" covering classification and license determination; reference to SOPs.

### 1.4 Recordkeeping

- **What it is:** Retention and reproducibility of export records per 15 C.F.R. Part 762 (five years from export or last transaction under license).
- **What to document:** What records are retained (classification memos, license determinations, screening, export docs); where and in what form; access and reproducibility.
- **ECP doc section:** "Recordkeeping" with citations to Part 762 and company retention policy.

### 1.5 Training

- **What it is:** Documented training for personnel who touch export decisions (sales, shipping, compliance, engineering as applicable).
- **What to document:** Who is trained, on what topics, how often; role-based depth; attestation or certification.
- **ECP doc section:** "Training" with reference to training outline and schedule.

### 1.6 Audits

- **What it is:** Periodic internal (and where appropriate external) review of classification accuracy, screening, license determinations, and recordkeeping.
- **What to document:** Audit frequency, scope, who performs it, how findings are remediated.
- **ECP doc section:** "Audits" with triggers and follow-up.

### 1.7 Handling violations and corrective action

- **What it is:** Process for identifying, reporting, and remediating potential violations; consideration of Voluntary Self-Disclosure (VSD) to BIS/OFAC per 15 C.F.R. § 764.5 and agency guidance.
- **What to document:** Who is notified, escalation to counsel, corrective action and retraining, VSD decision process.
- **ECP doc section:** "Handling Violations and Corrective Action."

### 1.8 Build and maintain the ECP manual

- **What it is:** The ECP document and linked SOPs are living documents; maintained, versioned, and accessible.
- **What to document:** Where the ECP and SOPs live; who updates them; review cycle (e.g. annual or upon material change).
- **ECP doc section:** "ECP Manual Maintenance."

### 1.9 Additional elements (tailored)

- **What it is:** Elements specific to organizational complexity (e.g. deemed exports, encryption annual reporting, ITAR-specific elements).
- **What to document:** Deemed export controls, ENC (b)(1) annual reporting if applicable, ITAR compliance program elements, third-party/customer compliance expectations.
- **ECP doc section:** "Company-Specific Elements" or "Additional Program Elements."

## 2. DDTC program expectations (ITAR)

For organizations with **ITAR** exposure (USML items, technical data, defense services):

- **Designation of responsible official** — Often a designated "Responsible Official" per DDTC practice; document role and authority.
- **Policies and procedures** — Written procedures for jurisdiction/classification, license applications (DSP-5, DSP-73, TAA, MLA), and compliance with license conditions.
- **Recordkeeping** — Per 22 C.F.R. Part 122 and contract/agreement terms; retention and availability for DDTC review.
- **Training** — Personnel handling ITAR items or technical data trained on ITAR requirements and company procedures.
- **Vulnerability to diversion / compliance reviews** — DDTC expects programs to address diversion risk and internal compliance reviews.

In the ECP document, include an **ITAR-specific** subsection under Export Authorization and under Additional Elements when the company’s product mix includes ITAR items or technical data. Reference 22 C.F.R. and DDTC guidance; do not substitute for legal advice.

## 3. ECP document structure (for template)

Recommended section order for the ECP Document Template:

1. **Document control** — Title, version, date, approval.
2. **Management commitment** — Policy statement; ECO designation; signatory.
3. **Scope and applicability** — Who and what the program covers (e.g. all exports under EAR; ITAR if applicable).
4. **Risk assessment** — How the company assesses and profiles risk.
5. **Export authorization** — Classification and license determination; pointer to SOPs.
6. **Recordkeeping** — Retention, location, reproducibility (15 C.F.R. Part 762).
7. **Training** — Who, what, how often; link to training outline.
8. **Audits** — Frequency, scope, remediation.
9. **Handling violations and corrective action** — Including VSD consideration.
10. **ECP manual maintenance** — Ownership, review cycle, version control.
11. **Company-specific / additional elements** — Deemed exports, encryption reporting, ITAR, CRM/ERP/agent integration.
12. **Integration with systems (optional)** — Where screening, classification, and licensing plug into CRM, ERP, or agents (when user provides systems).

Tailor each section from **company footprint**, **product mix**, and **risk profile** inputs. Use placeholders for company name, ECO title, jurisdictions (EAR/ITAR), and system names (CRM/ERP/agents).

## 4. Mapping to SOPs

The ECP document is **policy-level**; SOPs are **procedure-level**. The ECP should reference SOPs for:

- Denied party screening (when, how, tools, escalation).
- Classification (Order of Review, documentation, approval).
- License determination (five facts, Country Chart, exceptions, license applications).
- Recordkeeping (what to retain, where, how long).
- Escalation and triage (hold vs escalate, ECO/legal chain).

Each SOP should state purpose, scope, roles, steps, regulatory references (EAR/ITAR), and recordkeeping. When the user provides CRM/ERP/agent context, SOP steps can include "where this happens" (e.g. "Attach classification memo to opportunity in Salesforce"; "Run ExChek classification skill before quote release").
