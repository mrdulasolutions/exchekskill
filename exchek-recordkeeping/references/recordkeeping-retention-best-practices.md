# Recordkeeping and Retention Best Practices: 15 CFR Part 762 and ITAR

Reference for building a **retention schedule or checklist** under the EAR (15 C.F.R. Part 762) and, where applicable, the ITAR (22 C.F.R. Part 122). Use this to populate the Recordkeeping Retention Schedule and Checklist template and to tailor rows to the user's company activities (classification, license determinations, screening, shipments, deemed export, encryption, etc.) and jurisdiction (EAR only vs. EAR + ITAR).

---

## 1. 15 CFR Part 762 (EAR recordkeeping)

### § 762.2 — Records required

The EAR require retention of all records relating to transactions that are subject to the EAR. Such records include, but are not limited to:

- **Memoranda** — Classification memos, license determination memos, jurisdiction memos, deemed export review memos, red-flag assessments, escalation notes, self-audit reports.
- **Correspondence** — Emails, letters, and other communications relating to export authorization, screening, license applications, and compliance decisions.
- **Contracts** — Sales agreements, distribution agreements, and other contracts that reference export terms, re-export assurances, or compliance obligations.
- **Screening records** — Denied party screening results (parties screened, list(s) used, date, result — no hit / hit with list name and resolution); hit resolution documentation; adjudication rationales.
- **Export documentation** — Commercial invoices with export blocks, packing lists, shipper's letter of instruction (SLI), AES/EEI filing data or exemption documentation, bills of lading, export licenses and license exception citations.
- **License-related records** — License applications, approvals, provisos, and records of transactions conducted under a license or license exception.

Only include in the retention schedule the record types that correspond to the user's stated activities (e.g., if they do not perform deemed export reviews, omit deemed export memos or mark N/A).

### § 762.4 — Form of records

Records must be retained in a form that:

- Preserves the **original** record or a **reproduction** that is complete and legible.
- Is **reproducible** — the company must be able to produce the record for BIS (or DDTC for ITAR) upon request.
- Is **organized** so that any particular record can be **immediately located**.

In the retention schedule table, use a "Form / storage" column to state: e.g., "Originals or legible reproductions; reproducible upon request; organized and readily retrievable." The user may optionally specify a system (e.g., SharePoint, DMS); if provided, you can add a line such as "Stored in [user system] per company policy."

### § 762.6 — Retention period

Records required under Part 762 must be retained for **at least five years** from the **date of the export**, **reexport**, or **last transaction under a license**, whichever is **later**.

- For **classification memos** and **license determinations**: five years from the date of export or last transaction under the license to which they relate.
- For **screening records**: five years from the date of the transaction or export to which the screening relates.
- For **export documentation** (invoices, SLI, AES/EEI): five years from the date of export.
- For **deemed export review memos**, **red-flag assessments**, **escalation notes**: five years from the date of the release or transaction; align with program policy and § 762.6.

In the retention schedule, cite **15 C.F.R. § 762.6** for the retention period and state: "At least 5 years from date of export, reexport, or last transaction under license, whichever is later."

## 2. 22 CFR Part 122 (ITAR recordkeeping)

For organizations with **ITAR** exposure (USML items, technical data, defense services):

- **Applicability:** 22 C.F.R. Part 122 imposes recordkeeping requirements on registrants and persons involved in the manufacture, export, or temporary import of defense articles and defense services. Contract and agreement terms (e.g., TAA, MLA) may specify additional retention or availability for DDTC review.
- **Retention and availability:** Records must be retained and made available to DDTC upon request. Retention periods and form are set forth in Part 122 and in any applicable agreement (e.g., five years or as specified in the agreement).
- **Record types (ITAR):** License applications and approvals (DSP-5, DSP-73, etc.), TAAs, MLAs, technical data release records, manufacturing and export records, and correspondence relating to ITAR authorizations.

When the user selects **EAR + ITAR**, include a separate subsection or rows in the retention schedule for ITAR-related records and cite **22 C.F.R. Part 122** (and "contract/agreement terms" where applicable). When the user selects **EAR only**, omit ITAR rows or mark them "Not applicable — EAR only."

## 3. Record types by activity

Use the following to build the retention schedule table. **Include only rows for activities the user has selected** (and for ITAR rows only when jurisdiction is EAR + ITAR).

| Activity | Record type | Description | Retention (EAR) | Citation |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Classification** | Classification memos | Jurisdiction and ECCN/USML classification memos; technical exhibits; Order of Review documentation | 5 years from export or last transaction under license | 15 C.F.R. §§ 762.2, 762.6 |
| **Classification** | Jurisdiction determination memos | ITAR vs. EAR jurisdiction memos; CJ request supporting docs (if any) | Same as above | 15 C.F.R. Part 762; 22 C.F.R. Part 122 if ITAR |
| **License determinations** | License determination memos | Five determinative facts, Country Chart, exceptions, authorization path, DCS | 5 years from export or last transaction under license | 15 C.F.R. §§ 762.2, 762.6 |
| **License determinations** | License applications and approvals | BIS license applications and approvals; SNAP-R submissions; license provisos | Same as above | 15 C.F.R. Part 762 |
| **Screening** | Screening records | Denied party screening results (party, list(s), date, result); hit resolution; adjudication rationales | 5 years from date of transaction or export | 15 C.F.R. §§ 762.2, 762.6 |
| **Shipments** | Export documentation | Commercial invoice (export block), packing list, SLI, AES/EEI data or exemption; bills of lading | 5 years from date of export | 15 C.F.R. Part 762; Census FTR Part 30 for AES/EEI |
| **Shipments** | License exception / DCS records | Records supporting NLR or license exception (e.g., GBS, LVS) and destination control statement | 5 years from export or last transaction under license | 15 C.F.R. §§ 762.2, 762.6 |
| **Deemed export** | Deemed export review memos | Memos documenting deemed export analysis (734.2(b)); conclusion and supporting analysis | 5 years from date of release or transaction | 15 C.F.R. § 762.6 |
| **Encryption** | Encryption classification and notification records | 5A992/5D992 (or 5A002/5D002) classification; ENC/TSR eligibility; BIS/NSA notification or annual report prep (no filing) | 5 years from export or last activity; align with BIS guidance for ENC | 15 C.F.R. Part 762 |
| **Risk / escalation** | Risk triage and escalation notes | Risk scores; escalation recommendations; hold/release decisions | 5 years from date of transaction | 15 C.F.R. §§ 762.2, 762.6 |
| **Risk / escalation** | Red-flag assessment notes | BIS Know Your Customer red-flag checklist; overall assessment; escalation | Same as above | 15 C.F.R. § 762.6 |
| **Audit / lookback** | Self-audit and lookback reports | Retrospective audit reports; re-screening/re-classification findings; remediation | Retain per program; recommend at least 5 years | 15 C.F.R. Part 762 |
| **ITAR (if EAR + ITAR)** | ITAR license and agreement records | DSP-5, DSP-73, TAA, MLA, technical data releases; manufacturing/export records | Per 22 C.F.R. Part 122 and agreement terms | 22 C.F.R. Part 122 |

## 4. Tailoring the schedule

- **EAR only:** Include only rows for 15 C.F.R. Part 762. Omit ITAR row(s) or add one row "ITAR-related records" with value "Not applicable — EAR only."
- **EAR + ITAR:** Include all rows that match the user's activities, plus the ITAR row(s) and a brief regulatory summary that cites both Part 762 and Part 122.
- **Activities:** For each activity the user confirms (classification, license determinations, screening, shipments, deemed export, encryption, risk/escalation, audit/lookback), include the corresponding record type row(s). For activities they do not perform, omit the row or set "Not applicable" with a short reason (e.g., "We do not conduct deemed export reviews").
- **Checklist:** The optional "Do we retain this?" checklist in the template can list the same record types as the retention schedule table; user can check off internally.

## 5. Regulatory citations (for template summary)

- **EAR:** 15 C.F.R. Part 762 — Records to be kept (§ 762.2), form of records (§ 762.4), retention period (§ 762.6).
- **ITAR:** 22 C.F.R. Part 122 — Recordkeeping requirements for registrants and persons involved in defense articles and defense services; retention and availability for DDTC.
- **AES/EEI:** Census FTR Part 30 may impose additional retention for AES/EEI filing records; where the user has shipment/export doc activities, a brief note in the schedule is sufficient (e.g., "AES/EEI records retained per 15 C.F.R. Part 762 and Census FTR Part 30 as applicable").

Do not substitute for legal advice. Final retention policy and compliance with Part 762 and Part 122 are the responsibility of the user and their legal or compliance counsel.
