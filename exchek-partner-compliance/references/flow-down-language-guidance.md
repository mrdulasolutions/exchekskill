# Flow-Down Language: Guidance and Sample Clauses

Guidance for the optional "flow-down" annex of the Partner / Distributor Compliance Pack. These are **sample clauses only**; they do not constitute legal advice. Recommend that the user have all flow-down language reviewed by qualified legal or compliance counsel before use in any contract.

---

## Purpose of flow-down

Flow-down clauses contractually obligate the partner (distributor, reseller, OEM) to comply with U.S. export control laws and with the supplier’s stated requirements (screening, re-export assurances, recordkeeping). They support audit trails, due diligence, and remedy (including termination) if the partner breaches.

**EAR vs. ITAR:** If the supplier’s products are subject to the EAR only, flow-down should reference the EAR (15 C.F.R. Parts 730–774). If ITAR applies (USML items, technical data, defense services), include references to the ITAR (22 C.F.R. Parts 120–130) and to compliance with license or agreement conditions (e.g., TAA, MLA). Counsel can tailor for hybrid (EAR + ITAR) product mixes.

---

## Sample clauses (for annex only; recommend legal review)

The following are illustrative. Adapt to the agreement type (distribution, reseller, OEM) and to the supplier’s product mix and risk tolerance.

### Compliance with U.S. export laws

*Partner agrees to comply with all applicable U.S. export control and sanctions laws and regulations, including the Export Administration Regulations (EAR), 15 C.F.R. Parts 730–774, and, where applicable, the International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), 22 C.F.R. Parts 120–130. Partner shall not export, re-export, transfer, or otherwise dispose of any items received from Supplier to any country, person, or end-use except in compliance with such laws and regulations and with any authorization (license, license exception, or other approval) required thereunder.*

### No re-export or transfer without authorization

*Partner shall not re-export, transfer, or otherwise dispose of any items subject to the EAR (or ITAR, if applicable) to any destination, end-user, or end-use except as permitted by U.S. law. Partner shall obtain any required re-export or retransfer authorization before any such re-export or transfer and shall comply with all license or license exception conditions, including those relating to end-user, end-use, and reporting. Partner shall not alter or remove any Destination Control Statement or export control information provided by Supplier.*

### Screening obligations

*Partner shall screen itself, its customers, and any end-users or consignees against the U.S. government denial and sanctions lists (including but not limited to the BIS Consolidated Screening List, OFAC SDN and sanctions lists, and DDTC debarred parties where applicable) before onboarding and before each transaction involving items received from Supplier. Partner shall document screening results and retain such records in accordance with the recordkeeping requirements set forth below. Partner shall not sell, transfer, or ship to any party that appears on such lists or that is otherwise prohibited without prior authorization from the U.S. government.*

### Recordkeeping

*Partner shall retain all records relating to export compliance, including screening records, export and re-export documentation, and authorizations, for at least five (5) years from the date of export or re-export or the last transaction under a license, whichever is later, in accordance with 15 C.F.R. Part 762 (and 22 C.F.R. Part 122 where ITAR applies). Records shall be reproducible and legible and shall be made available to Supplier upon reasonable request for audit or compliance review.*

### Audit and cooperation

*Partner shall permit Supplier (or its designee) to review Partner’s export compliance records and procedures upon reasonable notice to verify compliance with this section. Partner shall cooperate in any investigation related to export compliance and shall promptly notify Supplier of any actual or potential violation of U.S. export laws in connection with items received from Supplier.*

### Termination for breach

*Material breach of the export compliance obligations under this section may be deemed a material breach of this agreement, entitling Supplier to suspend performance, terminate the agreement, or pursue other remedies in accordance with this agreement.*

## Use in the compliance pack

When the user requests "include flow-down language" or "include flow-down annex," the skill may:

1. Insert an annex (Section 4 or Annex A) in the Partner Distributor Compliance Pack template titled "Optional flow-down language" or "Sample contract clauses."
2. Populate the annex with clauses tailored from this guidance (e.g., select EAR-only vs. EAR+ITAR, and include screening, re-export, recordkeeping, audit, termination).
3. Add a clear disclaimer above the annex: *The following clauses are sample language only and do not constitute legal advice. Have all flow-down language reviewed by qualified legal or compliance counsel before use in any contract.*

This reference is for the skill’s use when generating the pack; it is not a substitute for legal advice.
