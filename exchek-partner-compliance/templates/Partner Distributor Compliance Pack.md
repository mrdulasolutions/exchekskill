## 1. Screening requirements

Partners must screen parties against U.S. government denial and sanctions lists and document results. This aligns with 15 C.F.R. § 732.4 and BIS "Know Your Customer" guidance (Supplement No. 3 to Part 732).

**When to screen:**

- **Before onboarding:** {{SCREENING_BEFORE_ONBOARDING}} (e.g., Screen the Partner entity and, if required by Supplier, key personnel or beneficial owners before approval of the partner relationship.)
- **Per transaction:** {{SCREENING_PER_TRANSACTION}} (e.g., Screen each customer, end-user, and any intermediate consignee before accepting an order or shipping items received from Supplier.)

**Lists and tools:** Screening must be conducted against the lists and tools specified by Supplier, which include at a minimum: {{SCREENING_LISTS}} (e.g., BIS Consolidated Screening List, OFAC SDN and applicable sanctions lists; DDTC debarred parties where ITAR items are involved). Partners may use {{SCREENING_TOOL_OR_SYSTEM}} or an equivalent that meets Supplier’s requirements.

**Documentation:** Partners must document each screening (party screened, date, list(s) used, result — no hit / hit with list name and resolution). Records must be retained per Section 3 and made available to Supplier upon reasonable request.

**Red flags:** Partners must be aware of and address BIS "Know Your Customer" red flags (Supplement No. 3 to Part 732). Where red flags are present, Partners must not proceed without resolution or escalation as required by Supplier’s policy or applicable law.

## 2. Re-export and transfer assurances

Items supplied by {{COMPANY_NAME}} may be subject to the EAR (and, where applicable, the ITAR). Re-exports and in-country transfers are subject to U.S. law (15 C.F.R. §§ 734.2, 736; 22 C.F.R. for ITAR).

**Partners agree and acknowledge that they will:**

- **No re-export or transfer without authorization:** Not re-export, transfer, or otherwise dispose of any items (in original form or incorporated into other products) to any country or person except in compliance with U.S. export control laws and with the required authorization (license, license exception, or No License Required (NLR), as applicable).
- **Destination Control Statement (DCS):** Comply with the Destination Control Statement (15 C.F.R. § 758.6) on export documentation. Partners shall not alter or remove the DCS or any export control information provided by Supplier.
- **License conditions:** Comply with all conditions of any license or license exception under which items were exported or re-exported (e.g., end-user, end-use, reporting, or re-export restrictions).
- **No diversion:** Not sell, transfer, or ship items to embargoed destinations, to parties on U.S. government denial or sanctions lists, or to prohibited end-users or end-uses without prior U.S. government authorization. Partners shall not facilitate diversion.

**Product / control level:** {{PRODUCT_CONTROL_LEVEL}} (e.g., Items supplied may include EAR99 and one or more ECCNs; all items are subject to the EAR. / Items may be subject to the ITAR; Partners must comply with ITAR re-export and retransfer requirements.)

## 3. Recordkeeping

Partners must retain export compliance records in accordance with U.S. law and Supplier’s requirements.

**What to retain:** Screening records; export and re-export documentation (e.g., commercial invoice with export block, packing list, Shipper’s Letter of Instruction, AES/EEI data if applicable); re-export authorizations (license exception citations or license numbers); and correspondence or determinations related to end-user/end-use. {{RECORDKEEPING_ADDITIONAL}}

**Retention period:** At least five (5) years from the date of export or re-export, or from the last transaction under a license, whichever is later (15 C.F.R. Part 762). Where ITAR applies, 22 C.F.R. Part 122 and any agreement terms may impose additional retention. {{RECORDKEEPING_STORAGE}}

**Format and access:** Records must be stored so they are reproducible in their original form and legible. Partners shall make records available to Supplier upon reasonable request for audit or compliance review.
