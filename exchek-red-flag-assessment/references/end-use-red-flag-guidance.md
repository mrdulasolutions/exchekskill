# End-use / End-user Red Flag Assessment Guidance

Structured checklist and assessment rules for BIS "Know Your Customer" red flags. Aligns with **Supplement No. 3 to 15 C.F.R. Part 732** and with exchek-skill-risk-triage escalation-best-practices and exchek-skill-csl Section 5 (Red Flag Assessment).

---

## 1. Authority and scope

- **BIS "Know Your Customer" Guidance:** Supplement No. 3 to 15 C.F.R. Part 732.
- **Purpose:** Run a structured checklist for a given party/transaction and produce an auditable red-flag assessment note (no / yes / conditional; escalate if needed).
- **Use:** Dedicated end-use/end-user review before committing; complements risk triage and denied-party screening. This skill does **not** perform screening, classification, or license determination.

---

## 2. Red flag checklist (BIS Supp. 3 to Part 732)

Assess each of the following for the party and transaction. For each item, determine **Present?** as **Yes**, **No**, or **Conditional**, and add **Notes** where relevant.

| # | Red flag | Guidance for Yes / No / Conditional |
|---|----------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 | **End-use inconsistent with product / counterparty unfamiliar with normal use** | Yes: stated end use does not match product capabilities, or counterparty cannot explain normal use. No: end use is consistent and counterparty is familiar. Conditional: under review or mitigated with documentation. |
| 2 | **Unwilling to provide end-use or end-user identity** | Yes: refusal or evasion. No: provided. Conditional: partial or pending. |
| 3 | **Unusual payment** (cash, third-party payment, overpayment) | Yes: structure is unusual and unexplained. No: standard commercial terms. Conditional: explained or mitigated. |
| 4 | **Destination differs from buyer country/billing without explanation** | Yes: shipping/billing mismatch with no explanation. No: consistent or explained. Conditional: explanation pending. |
| 5 | **Delivery instructions inconsistent with normal practice** | Yes: requests that deviate from normal commercial practice without explanation. No: normal. Conditional: under review. |
| 6 | **Requests removal of technical documentation** (manuals, safety data) | Yes: requested removal. No: not requested. Conditional: partial or clarified. |
| 7 | **No established business history or web presence** | Yes: no verifiable history or presence. No: established. Conditional: new entity with other mitigations. |
| 8 | **Country subject to comprehensive embargo or heightened controls** | Yes: destination is embargoed or subject to heightened controls. No: not. Conditional: transit only or license in place. |
| 9 | **Item controlled for AT, NP, CB, or MT reasons** | Yes: item is controlled for Anti-Terrorism, Nuclear, Chemical/Biological, or Missile Technology. No: not. Conditional: classification pending. |
| 10 | **Refused to provide ownership or identification (KYC)** | Yes: refusal to provide KYC/ownership docs. **Refusal is itself a red flag requiring escalation.** No: provided. Conditional: N/A for refusal—treat as Yes. |
| 11 | **Known or suspected ownership by Entity List / MEU / SDN (BIS Red Flag 29)** | Yes: any known or suspected ownership by listed entity. **Affirmative duty to determine ownership %.** No: no such ownership. Conditional: ownership trace in progress. |
| 12 | **Other** (specify) | Document any other relevant red flag; set Yes/No/Conditional and notes. |

---

## 3. Yes / No / Conditional

- **Yes:** The red flag is present or not adequately mitigated. Must be documented and typically leads to hold or escalate.
- **No:** The red flag is not present, or the situation is clearly benign and documented.
- **Conditional:** Under review, mitigated with specific conditions, or pending (e.g., ownership trace in progress). Document the condition and follow-up; disposition may be "Hold for export compliance" until resolved.

When in doubt, use **Conditional** and recommend follow-up rather than No.

---

## 4. Overall assessment

| Outcome | When to use |
|---------|-------------|
| **No red flags** | Every checklist item is No (and any "Other" is No or not applicable). No escalation recommended. |
| **Red flags present** | One or more items are Yes. Escalation or hold recommended per Section 5. |
| **Conditional** | One or more items are Conditional; no item is Yes. Recommend hold for compliance review or enhanced due diligence until resolved. |

Summarize in the assessment note with a short rationale (e.g., "No red flags identified; end use and counterparty documentation are consistent with product and policy." or "Red flags present: counterparty refused KYC; recommend escalate to legal.").

---

## 5. When to recommend escalation

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Any Yes** on refusal to provide ownership/KYC (item 10) | **Escalate to legal/compliance counsel.** Do not proceed without resolution. |
| **Any Yes** on known or suspected ownership by Entity List / MEU / SDN (item 11) | **Escalate to legal/compliance counsel.** Affirmative duty to determine ownership; do not proceed until resolved. |
| **Any other Yes** | **Hold for export compliance review** at minimum; escalate to legal if policy or risk warrants. |
| **One or more Conditional** | **Hold for export compliance review** or **enhanced due diligence** until resolved; then reassess. |
| **All No** | **No escalation.** May proceed per standard process (subject to screening/classification/license requirements). |

Document the recommendation in the assessment note: "No escalation" / "Hold for export compliance review" / "Escalate to legal/compliance counsel," with optional placeholders for "Escalated to," date, and follow-up.
